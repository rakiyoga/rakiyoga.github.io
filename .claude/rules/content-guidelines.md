# Content Guidelines

## Voice & Tone
- **Warm and welcoming**: Approachable, not clinical
- **Authoritative**: Grounded in traditional Ayurvedic texts
- **Educational**: Teach, don't preach
- **Inclusive**: Respect all health journeys

## Language
- Primary: English
- Sanskrit terms: Always include Devanagari script, Roman transliteration, and English meaning
- German: Include translations in parentheses where relevant for Berlin-based audience

## Health Disclaimers
Every page offering health advice must include or reference:
> "Our offerings do not replace a visit to a doctor. Please always consult a medical professional for health problems!"

## Content Structure
- Use expandable `<details>` sections for long educational content
- Bold key Ayurvedic terms on first use
- Use tables for comparative data (doshas, food classification, etc.)
- Include practical takeaways

## Imagery
- Use high-quality, relevant images of food, herbs, and nature
- Always provide descriptive alt text
- Credit sources when using external images
- Prefer own photography when available

## Accuracy
- Reference classical texts: Charaka Samhita, Sushruta Samhita, Ashtanga Hridayam
- Distinguish between traditional claims and scientifically verified information
- Never make absolute medical claims
- Present Ayurveda as complementary to modern medicine
